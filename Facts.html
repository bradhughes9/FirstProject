Facts about Cleveland

  1. Playhouse Square Center is the 2nd largest performing arts venue in the United States.
  2. The Cuyahoga River is also known as "The River that Caught Fire" because it has caught on fire 13 times.
  3. Lake Erie moderates the weather and temperature, keeping the city cooler in the summer and warmer in the winter, with the occasional lake effect with snow.
  4. In 1879, Cleveland became the first city to have electricity.
  5. Cleveland was also the first city in the United States to have an electrical traffic signal.
  6. The coldest temperature Cleveland ever reached was -20 degrees
  7. Cleveland's name comes from Moses Cleaveland, the "a" was removed because there wasn't enough room in the masthead
  8. Home of the Cleveland Clinic which has been the nations top heart program for nearly 20 years in a row.
  9. Superman was created in Cleveland in the 1930's
  10. Cleveland is known for it's art, hosting one of 14 international fashion showcases and the Cleveland Orchestra being recognized among the best orchestras in the world.
